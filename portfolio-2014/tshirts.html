Description

This was a event held by SFU-CSSA (Chinese Students and Scholars Association) in August 2013. They provided several booths in Richmond Night Market from August 30 to September 1, encouraged students to use the booths, come up with some startup idea, and start our own business in those 3 days.

My friends and I decided to sell personalized T-shirts. So we rent a heat transfer machine, bought heat transfer paper, and designed our own graphics. I was responsible for the graphic design part. I could also design custom graphics or print customers' own graphics when required.

As a result, lots of people in Richmond Night Market loved my graphic design. When they walked by our booth and saw the graphics on t-shirts, they would always laugh or smile, and pointed them to their family and friends to share their happiness.

Design Process

I started designing the graphics by drawing sketches, then use Adobe Illustrator to create a digital and polished version. After that, I created outlines for the graphics to make them printable. After printing them on the heat transfer paper, we use the heat transfer machine to transfer the graphics onto our T-shirts.
