Description

Singether is a desktop application prototype for a final project of my interface design course, done in collaboration with other 4 SIAT students.

Our objective was to create an interactive online karaoke software for linking and entertaining remote family members and friends.

Process and Outcome

At first, after brainstorming, our team came up with 3 software ideas that were valuable to be further developed for final project, and Singether was the one I came up with. We then held a group meeting to analyze each idea's pros and cons. After a discussion, we finally decided to take Singether as our final concept and further developed it.

During the development stage, my job was to design and optimize the software's UI/UX, as well as some of the graphics and transitions. While making decisions about the overall software flow, I need to be both logical and user-centered at the same time, in order to balance between a functional usability and fluent user experience.

After the Showcase Competition, Singether was invited to be showed at many other showcases afterwards. Each time it was complemented by lots of visitors, both on the interface design and the concept, which was very encouraging and meaningful to me.
