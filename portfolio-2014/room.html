Description

This realistic rendered room was done for an academic project of my 3D Animation course in SFU. My goal was to create a photo-realistic enviroment through modeling in Maya, and apply texture, light to make it more realistic.

Composition and Mood

My composition was a room interior with windows towards the south, so when the afternoon comes, the sunlight will falls into the room at a diagonal angel and warm the whole room. I wanted to create this vintage living room in a warm and calm afternoon. The weather is sunny but a little windy, as you can see from the flying curtains. The mood is relaxing, leisurely and cozy.

Modeling Process

Violin was the most difficult object to model in this project.

Violin body was started with its edge, by extruding a polygonal cube through a curve. Then I extruded the innere edge to make half of the violin body. I use boolean to make the F holes, and readjust the wiring and radian. Finally I used duplicate more to make the other half, reflected by y axis.

Violin scroll and neck was made from a single polygonal piece, rearrange points height to make the spiral shape, add surfaces, then extrude it.

Violin fingerboard was created by a polygonal cube, then use scale tool and move vertices to make the curved shape.

Violin bridge was started with a polygonal cube, delete other faces and keep only the front face. Then I rearranged the vertices and edges to make the outline and inner pattern, then extrude it to a 3D shape. After that, I used lattices to make the upper edge thinner, and then mirror it by x axis.

Violin peg was started with a polygonal cube, then delete other faces to keep 1 face. Then I rearrange the vertices, extrude, duplicate special to make the "head" of the peg. Then I make the long cylinder by using revolve, and make the little dot using polygonal sphere. After that, I align them using align tool, then group them together, and duplicate special to make the other 3 pegs.

Violin strings was modeled by creating Bezier curves and different radius circles first, then select both and extrude the 3D shape.

Sofa was started from a polygon cube, then use subdivisions, extrude, insert edge loop and basic transformation tools to adjust the shape. For the mattress, I followed this online tutorial to make it. The tool I used was basically poke face.

Wine bottles and wine glass was modeled by creating Bezier curves first, then use revolve tool to make the 3D shape.

Wine bottle label was created by a NURBS surface plane, then change it to a curved shape through controlling the vertices.

Curtains and the map were created using NURBS surface plane. I changed the shape by readjusting its vertices using soft selection.

Pillows were started from a polygon, subdivided it into 4x4x3, then move vertices to make the shape soft.

Walls, floor, and the carpet were created using polygon plane. The windows were created by subdividing the wall, removing faces, extruding and inserting edge loops.

Texture Materials

NOTE*: Since it is in mental ray mode, all the surface materials I used were Mia material x passes. However, the values and settings are set differently for matching their own materials.


Violin front body was using a wood violin texture with vertical bumps. I used plane UV to project to the front of the violin. It has little reflection, and glossiness value almost equals to 0.

Violin back body was using a wood violin back texture, then use plane UV to project it to the back of the violin. It has little reflection, and glossiness value almost equals to 0.

Violin finger board, tailpiece and chinrest was using a black wood material, with little reflection, glossiness value almost 0.

Violin bridge was using a light color wood texture and projected by plane UV. It has little reflection, and glossiness value almost equals to 0.

Violin strings was using default mia material x pass grey color, without using any texture. The reflection and glossiness are like 0.3.

Sofa was using a light green fabric texture. I wanted to use leather texture actually, but large and good leather texture is to difficult to find online. I decrease the reflection to about 0.2, with glossiness about 0.

Pillows were using 2 different fabric textures by spherical UV mapping. The reflection and glossiness are both about 0.

Walls were applied a purple vintage wallpaper texture to create the vintage looking and feeling. The reflection and glossiness are almost 0.

Window frame was using a white wood texture with a little bumps using 6 faces auto UV mapping. It has little reflection and the glossiness was almost 0.

Curtains were using the same fabric as the sofa, but with low reflection, low glossiness, high transparency and refraction of light green.

Map was using a vintage map image found online. The reflection and glossiness were both 0, to mimic the paper feeling.

Floor was applied a wood floor texture, with a little bit of reflection, and very low glossiness.

Carpet was applied a vintage carpet texture, with 0 reflection and 0 glossiness.

Wine bottleswas using the default mia material x pass, with high transparency and color of green and brown. Both have high refraction value. The reflection is almost 1 and glossiness is almost 1.

Wine glasses was using the default mia material x pass, with high transparency, clear color, and high refraction value. The reflection is almost 1 and glossiness is almost 1.

Wine bottle label was applied a wine label texture, with 0 reflection and 0 glossiness to mimic the paper feeling.

Lighting

Mental ray sunlight and sky was a directional light, positioned outside of the window, a little bit higher comparing to the window, pointing into the room at a angel about 30 degree. This light was the main light. I changed the Red/Blue shift to 0.300 to create this warm sunlight for the atmosphere of a warm and calm afternoon. The intensity was 7.

2 area lights were positioned right on the window and point into the room. The light source is from the outside, travel to the room through the windows. It is a sub key light to fill the sunlight. The color is white and intensity is 0.7.

Another area light was positioned inside the room on the left of the sofa, and pointing towards the sofa area. This light is a fill light to give some lights for the shadow created by sunlight and 2 area lights on the right.

Volumetric light was positioned from the window to the light area of the wall. It was in a box shape, and I use it to create the Tyndall effect, where you can see a little dust and fog under the sunshine.
