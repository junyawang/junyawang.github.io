Description

clipO is a concept that we developed for the "Innovation Prize" held by Purdue University, sponsored by Schurz Communications and Purdue University’s entrepreneurial hub The Foundry. It is an iOS based mobile application that aims to help video fans to produce high-quality videos with multiple perspectives, transition effects, and soundtrack using simple devices. It connects users who are interested in the same event or subject. By building connections, users will be able to share their perspectives, have access to more video resources, edit and then improve their work.

Process and Outcome

Since this is a U.S. located competition and the other 2 members were also in U.S., we brainstormed and developed ideas through online meeting.

As the project lead, I led the whole team forward and managed team members to ensure everyone finished his work flawlessly and on time.

As the only technical person and designer in the group, I was responsible for creating all the design and development work individually. With that in mind, I created a digital relationship diagram to analyze the app’s workflow and user experience logically, designed all the user interfaces and graphics individually using Illustrator, then created mockups in Photoshop. I also created an interactive prototype as a live demo to show how the application works. At last, I crafted a walkthrough motion graphic video for demo and promotion with After Effects, with self-designed graphics in Illustrator.

As a result, our project was nominated as one of the Top 5 teams in the competition. Although challenging myself to do all the design work was a tough process, it is still a remarkable experience for me with no doubt. I gained experience in leadership, management and communication skills by remotely cooperating with total non-designers. In addition, I also strengthened expertise on design process, proficency on Edge Animate and After Effects by self-training from online tutorials.
